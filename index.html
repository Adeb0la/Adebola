Choc Peanut Butter Fudge

TotalTime: 1 hrs 00 mins
Dietary Needs : Dairy Free, Gluten Free, Vegetarian
Rating: 4stars

Ingredients

1/2 cup natural peanut butter
1 tbs maple syrup
1/2 tsp vanilla bean paste
pinch of sea salt
3 tbs coconut oil, melted
1 chocolate truffle, finely chopped

  1. In a bowl combine peanut butter, maple syrup, vanilla, 2 tablespoons coconut oil and salt, mix until smooth.
  2. In a separate bowl mix 2 tablespoons of the peanut butter mixture with 1 tablespoon of coconut oil and the chocolate truffle. Blitz in the microwave for 15 secs and stir to combine.
  3. Pour the rest of the peanut butter mixture into a lined tin, alternately pours with the chocolate mixture. Create a swirl with the two using a knife or toothpick.
  4. Freeze for 1 hour to set.
  5. Repeat this process to make 3 layers.
  6. Allow the eggshells to set completely before turning out.
  7. Make the caramel by gently melting all the ingredients together until smooth, then chill in the fridge until firm.
  8. When you are ready to make the egg fill both chocolate halves with the caramel and stick together with melted chocolate and then chill again until needed.

  5. Place the golden caster sugar and water in a large heavy-based pan and stir constantly over a low heat with a wooden spoon for 3 to 5 minutes until the sugar has dissolved and there are no crystals.
  6. Add the butter and syrup mix to the pan and stir completely to mix. Bring to the boil with a sugar thermometer resting in the mixture.
  7. At this point, when the temperature on the sugar thermometer reaches 149° to 154°C work quickly to remove the pan from the heat then tilt the pan, and carefully dip the apples one at a time in the toffee.

Vegan Raspberry Chocolate Recipe

  1. Melt the coconut oil gently in the microwave, heating in short bursts of 10-15 seconds.
  2. Add in the cocoa powder, maple syrup, Truvia and vanilla extract to the coconut oil. Stirring quickly to combine so the mixture is smooth.
  3. Drop a teaspoon of the chocolate mixture into each section of a silicone mould or ice cube tray. Place one raspberry on top of the chocolate mixture, then top with another spoonful of chocolate to cover the raspberries.
  4. Place the chocolates into the freezer for 10-15 minutes until set. You can also set in the fridge but it will take longer to set.
  5. Pop the chocolates out of the mould and serve immediately, or store in the fridge until ready to serve.

Millionaire’s Shortbread Recipe

  1. Heat the oven to 160°C (fan 140°C, gas mark 3). Line a 20cm x 23cm tin with baking paper, making sure the paper overhangs slightly so it makes it easier to remove finished cake .
  2. For the shortbread layer, place the sugar, butter and flours into a food processor, whiz until soft dough is made. Spoon into the prepared tin and press lightly to form a smooth base.
  3. Chill for about 20 minutes and then bake for 35 minutes or until golden. Leave to cool.
  4. To make the caramel, place the butter, sugar, syrup and condensed milk into a saucepan and stir over a low heat until the butter has melted.
  5. Bubble and stir the mixture gently for five to eight minutes, until you get a thick and fudge-like mix. Pour over the cold shortbread in an even layer. Leave to cool.
  6. Once the caramel has cooled, chop the chocolate and place in a bowl suspended over a pan of simmering water. Do not let the water touch the bottom of the pan. Stir the chocolate until melted. Spread evenly over the caramel and allow to set.
  7. When ready to serve remove from the tin using the paper to help and cut into little squares.
